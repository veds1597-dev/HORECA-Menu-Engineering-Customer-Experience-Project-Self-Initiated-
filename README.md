1. Project Objective
To analyze how two leading Indian buffet brands—Barbeque Nation and Absolute Barbecues—drive customer satisfaction through menu engineering, service experience, and value proposition, and to identify improvement opportunities for better customer retention and sales performance in the HORECA channel.

2. Scope of Study
Menu engineering analysis

Live-grill experience comparison

Customer experience (CX) mapping

Dessert section analysis

Value-for-money comparison

Observational study inside 2–3 outlets

3. Methodology
a) Outlet Visits
Visited one outlet each of Barbeque Nation and Absolute Barbecues in Bangalore during peak hours.

b) Observation Checklist
Evaluated:

Menu design & variety

Customization options

Live-grill performance

Staff service quality

Ambience & crowd management

Dessert spread & presentation

Engagement activities

c) Customer Feedback
Spoke informally with 5–7 customers at both outlets to understand preferences.

d) Menu Engineering Framework Used
Categorized items as:

Stars (high popularity + high margin)

Plowhorses (high popularity + low margin)

Puzzles (low popularity + high margin)

Dogs (low popularity + low margin)

4. Key Findings
A. Barbeque Nation – Strengths
Excellent dessert section with high variety (Brownies, Kulfi nation, cakes).

Strong live-grill flavors satisfying mass Indian palate.

Better vegetarian spread.

Quick staff responsiveness.

A. Barbeque Nation – Weaknesses
Limited exotic food options.

Standardized experience, lacks “excitement factor.”

B. Absolute Barbecues – Strengths
Wider variety including exotic meats & global flavours.

Live counter customizations are a major highlight.

Attractive ambience, more premium feel.

Customers prefer it for celebrations & special occasions.

Absolute Barbecues – Weaknesses
Dessert section is weaker—less variety & lower visual appeal.

Longer waiting times during peak hours.

5. Comparative Insights (CX + Menu Engineering)
Customer Experience
Barbeque Nation: More family-friendly, consistency-focused.

AB’s: More experiential, adventure-food driven.

Menu Engineering
Barbeque Nation:

Stars → Desserts, Grills

Plowhorses → Indian starters

Dogs → Exotic items (few)

Absolute Barbecues:

Stars → Custom grill, exotic meats

Plowhorses → Indian dishes

Puzzles → Desserts

6. Recommendations for Both Brands
For Barbeque Nation
Introduce limited-time exotic items.

Add personalized grill options to match AB’s.

Modernize menu design (QR/menu board mix).

For Absolute Barbecues
Strengthen dessert section to match customer expectations.

Better queue management to reduce waiting time.

Introduce loyalty program to boost repeat visits.

For Both Brands
Implement cross-selling & upselling training for staff.

Use menu engineering insights to highlight high-margin items.

Improve digital engagement & pre-booking experience.

7. Conclusion
This comparison highlights how menu engineering, dessert variety, customization options, and customer experience directly influence customer satisfaction in the buffet restaurant segment.
The insights can be used by HORECA brands, OOH sales teams, and foodservice companies to design better menus, pricing strategies, and customer engagement models.
